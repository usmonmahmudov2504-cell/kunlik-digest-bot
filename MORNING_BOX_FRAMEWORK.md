# 📦 Morning Box — Content Generation Framework

Internal editorial system for the Morning Box AI Content Director.
Every post is generated and published in **Uzbek (Latin script)**. This document is the
operating manual — each category below is its own editorial guideline.

---

## Global rules (apply to every category)

- **One idea per post.** Never mix two big ideas in one piece.
- **Structure (always):** (1) Why does this matter? → (2) How does it work? → (3) How can the reader use it *today*? → end with **one practical action**.
- **Truth first:** verify before writing. Prefer books, research papers, universities, official company blogs, primary sources. **Never invent statistics, quotes, names, or dates.** If unsure, stay general — do not fabricate.
- **Original always:** never copy or lightly reword another Telegram post. Rewrite from understanding.
- **Tone:** premium, minimal, professional, calm, elegant, trustworthy. No clickbait, no spam, no exaggeration.
- **Default length:** 130–220 words (`blog_words`). Category overrides below.
- **Formatting:** plain text only — no HTML, markdown, or `*`. Max 1–3 relevant emojis. Strong first line. No "Title:" labels.
- **Quality bar:** every post should feel worthy of Morning Brew, Huberman Lab, HBR, Farnam Street, Blinkist, James Clear, Ali Abdaal.

## 1. Book Box

1. **Purpose:** Distill one powerful, actionable lesson from a real, well-known book.
2. **Target emotion:** Curiosity + "I need to read/apply this."
3. **Writing framework:** Big idea → core mechanism the author explains → one concrete way to apply it today.
4. **Recommended length:** 160–220 words.
5. **Best sources:** The actual book, author interviews, university summaries, Blinkist-style abstracts (verified, not copied).
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Confirm author + title are real and correctly paired; only quote if the wording is certain; otherwise paraphrase the idea.
7. **Formatting rules:** Name the book + author once, early. No long plot summary. End with the lesson, not the synopsis.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu g'oyani bitta qaroringda sinab ko'r." / "Ushbu kitobni o'qiganmisiz? Asosiy saboqni izohda yozing."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal book spine or single open book on a calm gradient; one short idea-phrase overlaid.
10. **Common mistakes:** Generic "this book is great"; fabricated quotes; summarizing the whole book instead of one usable lesson.

## 2. Mind Box (Psychology)

1. **Purpose:** Explain one principle of human behavior or the mind that improves self-awareness.
2. **Target emotion:** "Oh — that's why I do that."
3. **Writing framework:** Name the effect/bias → why the brain does it → how to notice and counter it today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Peer-reviewed psychology, Kahneman/Cialdini-level work, university psych departments.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Use only established, replicated effects; avoid pop-psych myths (e.g., "left/right brain"); no invented study numbers.
7. **Formatting rules:** Name the concept clearly; one relatable example (general, not a fake personal story).
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu his-tuyg'u kelsa, bir nafas ol va nomini ayt." / "Keyingi safar buni o'zingda kuzating."
9. **Cover image concept:** Abstract brain/mind motif or two-path silhouette; muted, premium palette.
10. **Common mistakes:** Fake "studies show X%"; turning psychology into horoscope-style claims; fabricated anecdotes.

## 3. Money Box

1. **Purpose:** Build financial literacy and a healthy money mindset.
2. **Target emotion:** Calm control + "I can manage money better."
3. **Writing framework:** Principle → the math/mechanism behind it → one small money habit to start today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Personal-finance classics (Morgan Housel, Bogle), central bank/IMF basics, reputable finance education.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** No specific investment promises or fake returns; verify any rate/figure or omit it; never give risky "guaranteed" advice.
7. **Formatting rules:** Concrete but not preachy; no get-rich-quick framing.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun bitta keraksiz xarajatni belgilab, uni jamg'armaga yo'naltir." / "Daromadingning 1 foizini bugun chetga qo'y."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal coin/jar/growth-curve icon; restrained gold accent on dark.
10. **Common mistakes:** Hype, fake numbers, "this stock will…", moralizing about poverty/wealth.

## 4. Business Box

1. **Purpose:** Teach one durable principle of building or growing a business.
2. **Target emotion:** Strategic clarity + ambition.
3. **Writing framework:** Principle/model → why it works → how a small founder applies it this week.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–220 words.
5. **Best sources:** HBR, Stratechery-level analysis, founder memoirs, official company blogs/case studies.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify company facts (founders, dates, outcomes); don't exaggerate "X grew 1000x" without proof; localize for Uzbek reality.
7. **Formatting rules:** One model per post; concrete example; practical takeaway, not theory dump.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun biznesingda shu savolga javob ber: kim sening haqiqiy mijozing?" / "Ushbu modelni o'z loyihangga moslab ko'r."
9. **Cover image concept:** Clean geometric framework diagram or upward path; corporate-minimal.
10. **Common mistakes:** Buzzword soup; survivorship bias; copying foreign advice without local context.

## 5. Marketing Box

1. **Purpose:** Reveal how attention, persuasion, and customer psychology actually work.
2. **Target emotion:** "I finally understand why people buy."
3. **Writing framework:** Insight → the psychology/mechanism → one tactic to test today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Cialdini, Ogilvy, Seth Godin, real brand case studies, official marketing blogs.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify case-study claims; no invented conversion stats; distinguish principle from anecdote.
7. **Formatting rules:** Lead with the customer's mind, not the product; one tactic, clearly explained.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun o'z taklifingni mijoz ehtiyoji tilida qayta yoz." / "Sarlavhangni 3 xil variantda sinab ko'r."
9. **Cover image concept:** Magnet, eye, or funnel motif; bold-but-clean single accent color.
10. **Common mistakes:** "Growth hacks" with no substance; fake metrics; confusing manipulation with persuasion.

## 6. AI Box

1. **Purpose:** Make AI practical and understandable, with real ways to use it today.
2. **Target emotion:** Empowerment, not fear.
3. **Writing framework:** What it is/does → how it works (plain words) → one concrete way to use it today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Official model/provider docs and blogs (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google), reputable AI research, university courses.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify model names/capabilities are current; no hype or sci-fi claims; mark uncertainty honestly.
7. **Formatting rules:** Explain jargon in one line; show a usable prompt or workflow when possible.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun bitta takroriy ishni AI'ga top — va vaqtingni o'lcha." / "Shu promptni o'zingda sinab ko'r."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal neural/spark motif; cool premium palette, no cliché robots.
10. **Common mistakes:** Overhyping AGI; outdated model facts; vague "AI will change everything" with no action.

## 7. Technology Box

1. **Purpose:** Explain a technology or future skill that matters for the reader's life/career.
2. **Target emotion:** Forward-looking curiosity + readiness.
3. **Writing framework:** What's changing → why it matters → one skill or step to prepare today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Official product docs, reputable tech journalism, standards bodies, primary announcements.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify specs/dates from primary sources; separate fact from speculation; no rumor-as-fact.
7. **Formatting rules:** Plain-language explanation; tie to the reader's real life or work.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu vositani 10 daqiqa sinab ko'r." / "Keyingi 6 oyda o'rganmoqchi bo'lgan bitta ko'nikmani yoz."
9. **Cover image concept:** Clean circuit/device line-art; restrained, modern.
10. **Common mistakes:** Spec-dumping; gadget hype; ignoring "so what for me."

## 8. Islamic Wisdom

1. **Purpose:** Share authentic Islamic wisdom that gives meaning, ethics, and inner balance.
2. **Target emotion:** Peace, reflection, sincerity.
3. **Writing framework:** Wisdom/principle → its meaning → how to live it today.
4. **Recommended length:** 130–190 words.
5. **Best sources:** Qur'an and authentic (sahih) hadith with correct attribution, recognized scholars, classical works.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** **Highest care** — only authentic, correctly attributed sources; never invent or paraphrase a hadith as if quoting; if unsure, present as general wisdom, not scripture.
7. **Formatting rules:** Respectful, humble tone; no sectarian disputes; no rulings/fatwa beyond well-known basics.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu amalni bir marta niyat bilan bajar." / "Bugun bir kishiga chin ko'ngildan yaxshilik qil."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal geometric/arabesque pattern, calm earth tones; no figurative imagery.
10. **Common mistakes:** Misattributed or weak hadith presented as authentic; preachy tone; controversial rulings.

## 9. English Box

1. **Purpose:** Teach one genuinely useful English word, phrase, or idiom with real usage.
2. **Target emotion:** "I can use this today."
3. **Writing framework:** Word/phrase → meaning + when to use it → one example sentence + a learning tip.
4. **Recommended length:** 110–170 words (shorter is fine).
5. **Best sources:** Oxford/Cambridge/Merriam-Webster dictionaries, corpora, reputable ESL resources.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify spelling, meaning, and natural usage; no made-up idioms; ensure the Uzbek translation is accurate.
7. **Formatting rules:** Show the English clearly; give 1 example sentence; keep Uzbek explanation simple.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu iborani bitta jumlada ishlat." / "Shu so'zni eslab qolish uchun o'zing bitta gap tuz."
9. **Cover image concept:** Clean typographic card with the word large; bilingual minimal layout.
10. **Common mistakes:** Rare/archaic words; wrong usage; literal mistranslation; overloading with too many words.

## 10. Arabic Box

1. **Purpose:** Teach one useful Arabic word/phrase and its meaning, opening the language and culture.
2. **Target emotion:** Discovery + connection.
3. **Writing framework:** Word/phrase (with transliteration) → meaning → where it's used + how to remember it.
4. **Recommended length:** 110–170 words.
5. **Best sources:** Reputable Arabic dictionaries, recognized language resources, Classical/MSA references.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify spelling, harakat, transliteration, and meaning; distinguish Classical vs. dialect; no fabricated etymology.
7. **Formatting rules:** Give script + transliteration + Uzbek meaning; one short example.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu so'zni ovoz chiqarib 3 marta takrorla." / "Shu iborani bilganmidingiz? Izohda yozing."
9. **Cover image concept:** Elegant Arabic calligraphy of the word on a calm background.
10. **Common mistakes:** Wrong transliteration; mixing dialects; inventing root meanings; cultural insensitivity.

## 11. History Box

1. **Purpose:** Draw one timeless lesson from a real historical event or figure.
2. **Target emotion:** Perspective + "history rhymes."
3. **Writing framework:** The event/figure → what actually happened → the lesson for today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Academic history, primary sources, university/museum material, reputable historians.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify dates, names, sequence; avoid myths and disputed legends as fact; note uncertainty where it exists.
7. **Formatting rules:** Brief, accurate setup; focus on the lesson, not a full chronicle.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu saboqni o'z hayotingga qanday bog'laysan?" / "Tarix takrorlanmasligi uchun bugun nima qilasan?"
9. **Cover image concept:** Vintage-toned minimal motif (map, column, old document); muted sepia accent.
10. **Common mistakes:** Popular myths stated as fact; wrong dates; nationalism/bias; moralizing.

## 12. Leadership Box

1. **Purpose:** Teach one principle of leading people and building strong teams.
2. **Target emotion:** Responsibility + capability.
3. **Writing framework:** Principle → why it builds trust/results → one leadership behavior to practice today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Leadership research, HBR, respected leaders' verified writing, military/sports/business case studies.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify any attributed leadership story; no fabricated quotes from famous leaders; localize.
7. **Formatting rules:** Behavior-focused, not slogan-focused; concrete situation.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun jamoangdagi bir kishini chin ko'ngildan maqta." / "Bugun bitta qarorni jamoang bilan maslahatlash."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal compass, lighthouse, or front-of-group silhouette; strong, calm.
10. **Common mistakes:** Empty motivational quotes; fake leader stories; "boss vs leader" clichés with no substance.

## 13. Communication Box

1. **Purpose:** Improve how the reader speaks, listens, and persuades.
2. **Target emotion:** Confidence in being understood.
3. **Writing framework:** Communication insight → why it works psychologically → one technique to use in the next conversation.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Communication research, negotiation experts (e.g., Voss), public-speaking and rhetoric classics.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Use established techniques; no invented "studies"; keep examples realistic.
7. **Formatting rules:** Show the technique in a concrete phrasing the reader can copy.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugungi suhbatda kamroq gapir, ko'proq tingla." / "Bugun bir kishini gapini bo'lmasdan oxirigacha eshit."
9. **Cover image concept:** Speech-bubble/sound-wave minimal motif; warm but clean.
10. **Common mistakes:** Manipulative "tricks"; vague advice ("just be confident"); fake dialogue examples.

## 14. Critical Thinking

1. **Purpose:** Sharpen reasoning and protect the reader from bad thinking.
2. **Target emotion:** Mental clarity + intellectual independence.
3. **Writing framework:** A reasoning trap/skill → how it distorts thinking → one question to ask yourself today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Logic/critical-thinking texts, cognitive science, Farnam Street, philosophy basics.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Define fallacies/biases correctly; use accurate examples; no strawmen.
7. **Formatting rules:** Give the reader a reusable mental question, not just a label.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun bir fikringga 'qaerdan bilaman?' deb savol ber." / "Keyingi xabarni o'qiganda manbasini so'ra."
9. **Cover image concept:** Magnifying glass / branching-question motif; clean, intellectual.
10. **Common mistakes:** Naming a fallacy without explaining; using it to "win arguments"; condescension.

## 15. Mental Models

1. **Purpose:** Give one reusable thinking tool that improves decisions across life.
2. **Target emotion:** "I have a new lens for the world."
3. **Writing framework:** Name the model → the core idea → where it applies + how to use it on a real decision today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Farnam Street, Munger's latticework, the original discipline (physics, biology, economics) the model comes from.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Attribute the model's origin correctly; don't misdefine it; give an accurate application.
7. **Formatting rules:** One model per post; clear name; transferable example.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugungi bitta qarorni shu model orqali ko'rib chiq." / "Shu modelni qaysi muammoga qo'llaysan?"
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal lattice/lens/gear motif; intellectual-premium.
10. **Common mistakes:** Listing many models shallowly; misusing the model; no concrete application.

## 16. Habits

1. **Purpose:** Help the reader build one good habit (or break a bad one) with real mechanics.
2. **Target emotion:** "I can actually change this."
3. **Writing framework:** Habit principle (cue–routine–reward, identity) → why it works → one tiny step to start today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** James Clear (Atomic Habits), Charles Duhigg, behavioral science, Stanford BJ Fogg.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Use accurate habit science; avoid the "21 days" myth; no fake percentages.
7. **Formatting rules:** Emphasize *small and specific*; one concrete starting action.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu odatni 2 daqiqalik versiyada boshla." / "Ertangi kun uchun bitta kichik odat belgila."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal chain/streak/seed-growth motif; calm and encouraging.
10. **Common mistakes:** "21 days to a habit" myth; overwhelming plans; willpower-only advice.

## 17. Productivity

1. **Purpose:** Help the reader do meaningful work with focus, not just "more."
2. **Target emotion:** Calm focus + control over time.
3. **Writing framework:** Productivity principle → why it beats hustle → one method to apply today.
4. **Recommended length:** 140–200 words.
5. **Best sources:** Cal Newport (Deep Work), Ali Abdaal, time-management research, GTD.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Avoid fake "average person wastes X hours" stats; keep methods evidence-based.
7. **Formatting rules:** One method, clearly actionable; respect the reader's time (be concise).
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun eng muhim bitta ishni birinchi qil." / "Telefoningni 1 soat boshqa xonaga qo'y va ishla."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal focus/clock/single-task motif; clean, calming.
10. **Common mistakes:** Glorifying busyness; tool-hopping; unrealistic systems; fake statistics.

## 18. Success Stories

1. **Purpose:** Tell a real story of a person/company and extract a usable lesson.
2. **Target emotion:** Inspiration grounded in reality.
3. **Writing framework:** Who + real situation → the key decision/struggle → the lesson the reader can apply.
4. **Recommended length:** 160–220 words.
5. **Best sources:** Verified biographies, official interviews, reputable journalism, company history.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Verify the person, facts, and outcome; **no fabricated stories or quotes**; show struggle, avoid survivorship bias.
7. **Formatting rules:** Story → lesson; keep it honest (include the hard part, not just the win).
8. **CTA examples:** "Shu hikoyadan o'zingga bitta saboq olib, bugun qo'lla." / "Sen qaysi qadamdan boshlaysan?"
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal portrait silhouette or summit motif; warm, aspirational, premium.
10. **Common mistakes:** Invented or exaggerated stories; "overnight success" myths; lesson-free hero worship.

## 19. Interesting Facts

1. **Purpose:** Share one verified, genuinely fascinating fact that makes the reader think.
2. **Target emotion:** Delight + "I want to share this."
3. **Writing framework:** The fact → why it's true / how it works → why it matters or what it teaches.
4. **Recommended length:** 110–170 words.
5. **Best sources:** Scientific journals, universities, encyclopedias, primary research.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** **Verify rigorously** — these spread fast; one wrong fact damages trust; cite the mechanism, not just the claim; no "fun facts" myths.
7. **Formatting rules:** Lead with the fact; add the meaning; never leave it as trivia only.
8. **CTA examples:** "Buni bilarmidingiz? Yana qiziq faktni izohda yozing." / "Bugun shu faktni bir kishiga aytib ber."
9. **Cover image concept:** Single bold visual of the fact's subject; minimal, intriguing.
10. **Common mistakes:** Viral myths (Great Wall from space, etc.); no source; trivia with no insight.

## 20. Life Lessons

1. **Purpose:** Offer one piece of grounded wisdom that helps the reader live better.
2. **Target emotion:** Quiet resonance + reflection.
3. **Writing framework:** Observation/principle → why it's true → how to live it today.
4. **Recommended length:** 130–190 words.
5. **Best sources:** Philosophy (Stoicism), wisdom literature, reflective essays, lived universal truths.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Honest reflection only — **no fabricated personal stories** ("a friend of mine…"); general observations are fine, fake events are not.
7. **Formatting rules:** Sincere, human, calm; strong first line; reflective closing question or small action.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugun shu fikr bilan bir lahza to'xtab o'yla." / "Bugun bir kishidan kechirim so'ra yoki rahmat ayt."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal nature/path/horizon motif; soft, contemplative tones.
10. **Common mistakes:** Empty platitudes; fake personal anecdotes; preachy moralizing.

## 21. Decision Making

1. **Purpose:** Help the reader make better, clearer decisions under uncertainty.
2. **Target emotion:** Confidence + reduced anxiety.
3. **Writing framework:** Decision principle/tool → why it improves choices → apply it to one real decision today.
4. **Recommended length:** 150–210 words.
5. **Best sources:** Kahneman, Annie Duke (Thinking in Bets), decision science, mental-model literature.
6. **Fact-check workflow:** Use accurate decision concepts; no fake probabilities; separate process from outcome.
7. **Formatting rules:** Give a reusable rule or question; one concrete decision to test it on.
8. **CTA examples:** "Bugungi bitta qarorni 'eng yomon holatda nima bo'ladi?' savoli bilan ko'rib chiq." / "Qaroringni 10/10/10 qoidasi bilan sinab ko'r."
9. **Cover image concept:** Minimal crossroads/fork or weighing-scale motif; clear, balanced.
10. **Common mistakes:** Judging decisions only by outcome; fake probabilities; analysis-paralysis advice with no action.

## How this maps to the bot

- Each category here corresponds to a rotating theme in `channels.json → Morning Box → blog_themes`.
- The global rules above are already encoded in `voice_persona` + `voice_focus`.
- Per-post length follows `blog_words: [130, 220]`, with category overrides as a soft guide.
- The AI never invents facts/quotes; uncertain → general. This is enforced in the blog prompt and reinforced here.
